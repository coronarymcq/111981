Q1: What is the MOST likely reason why patient safety is now seen as more than just preventing errors?

(A) Errors are rare in modern clinical practice
(B) Not all patient harm is caused by errors
(C) Most errors are always preventable
(D) Harm only occurs due to negligence

Correct Answer: (B)
(A) Incorrect – Errors are still common.
(B) Correct – Harm can occur even without errors (e.g., complications, nosocomial infections).
(C) Incorrect – Many errors are not preventable.
(D) Incorrect – Harm is not always linked to negligence.

Q2: A retrospective review of medical records finds frequent adverse drug events in outpatients. What percentage of these events are typically classified as SERIOUS?

(A) 1%
(B) 6%
(C) 15%
(D) 25%

Correct Answer: (B)
(A) Incorrect – Too low.
(B) Correct – 6% of patients experienced serious drug-related events like bradycardia or GI bleeding.
(C) & (D) Incorrect – Overestimate the actual figure.

Q3: Which of the following best describes an adverse event in the healthcare system?

(A) Any unexpected clinical deterioration
(B) Unintended injury caused by medical management rather than disease
(C) Harm caused solely by hospital-acquired infections
(D) Complication that results in readmission

Correct Answer: (B)
(A) Incorrect – Too vague.
(B) Correct – This is the formal definition of an adverse event.
(C) Incorrect – Too narrow; not all adverse events are infections.
(D) Incorrect – Not all readmissions are adverse events.

Q4: Which component is NOT one of the "three pillars" of patient safety?

(A) Avoidance
(B) Prevention
(C) Compliance
(D) Amelioration

Correct Answer: (C)
(A), (B), (D) – All are true pillars.
(C) Incorrect – While important, compliance is not one of the foundational concepts of patient safety.

Q5: Which factor makes defining harm in healthcare particularly difficult compared to other industries?

(A) Harm can arise from treatment or the disease itself
(B) Errors are rarely documented
(C) There is no existing reporting infrastructure
(D) Most adverse events occur outside the hospital

Correct Answer: (A)
(A) Correct – It's difficult to separate harm due to healthcare from disease itself.
(B) Incorrect – Errors are reported, but harm can occur without errors.
(C) Incorrect – Reporting systems exist but vary.
(D) Incorrect – Many events occur within the hospital.

Q6: A patient was readmitted 5 days after discharge due to bleeding from an anticoagulant. This is an example of:

(A) Adverse drug event
(B) Nosocomial infection
(C) Preventable complication
(D) Surgical site infection

Correct Answer: (A)
(A) Correct – This is a classic adverse drug event (ADE).
(B) Incorrect – Not an infection.
(C) Incorrect – Could be preventable, but it’s better classified as ADE.
(D) Incorrect – No surgery involved.

Q7: Which of the following is a strength of regional or national reporting systems?

(A) Provides rapid feedback to bedside clinicians
(B) Efficient for analyzing single incidents in depth
(C) Detects patterns of rare incidents across institutions
(D) Avoids the need for written reports

Correct Answer: (C)
(A) & (B) Incorrect – Local systems are better for rapid feedback and deep analysis.
(C) Correct – Regional/national systems detect trends across many institutions.
(D) Incorrect – Written reports are essential for data collection.

Q8: Which of the following is the BEST example of a system-level change proposed by Lucian Leape?

(A) Disciplining physicians who make errors
(B) Redesigning work environments to reduce error
(C) Increasing the workload to improve vigilance
(D) Mandating perfect performance through training

Correct Answer: (B)
(A) & (D) – These rely on outdated "perfectibility model."
(B) Correct – Leape promoted system changes like better workflow and environment.
(C) Incorrect – Higher workload increases, not reduces, errors.

Q9: A surgical patient in the UK develops complications, but survives. What proportion of such complications are believed to be avoidable?

(A) 10–15%
(B) 20–30%
(C) 30–50%
(D) 60–75%

Correct Answer: (C)
(A) & (B) – Underestimate the reality.
(C) Correct – Studies show 30–50% of surgical complications are avoidable.
(D) – Overestimate.

Q10: Why are elderly patients more vulnerable to harm in hospitals compared to younger patients?

(A) Elderly patients do not respond to treatment
(B) Elderly patients receive fewer interventions
(C) They have multiple comorbidities and decreased physiological reserve
(D) They are usually misdiagnosed

Correct Answer: (C)
(A) Incorrect – Many elderly respond well to treatment.
(B) Incorrect – They often receive multiple interventions.
(C) Correct – Their physiologic fragility and polypharmacy increase risk.
(D) Incorrect – Misdiagnosis is not the primary vulnerability factor.
